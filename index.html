What are Pallas's cats?

Pallas's cats are is a small sized wild cat with long thick fur and round ears.

The Pallas cat has very small legs to sneak up on their prey without being seen and It has a long bushy tail.

The cat is camoflauged well with thick bushy fur in the colour of their environment and has adapted to the cold climates it habitates.

The Pallas's cat was described first in 1776 by Peter Simon Pallas who observed the cat near Lake Baikal.


What is their habitat?

The Pallas cat is usually found in mountains and grasslands in Northern India, Mongolia, Iran, Pakistan and Afghanistan they are also found in dry rocky areas and their fur is used to camoflauge within the environment.

They also live in snowy-top mountains

Life expectancy and adulthood.

In the wild Pallas cats live up to 8 or 9 years, some of the pallas cats kept in captivity lived to around 14 years old! (average life expectancy of a domesticated cat.)
